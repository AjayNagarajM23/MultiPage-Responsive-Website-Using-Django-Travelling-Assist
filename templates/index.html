                                           UTTARA KANNADA

Uttara Kannada district is one of the largest districts of the state and has abundant natural resources. The district with its distinct geographical features comprises of dense forest, perennial rivers, abundant flora and fauna and a long coastline. The district has 12 taluks namely Karwar, Ankola, Kumata, Bhatkala, Honnavar, Siddapur, Shirsi, Yallapur, Mundagoda, Supa (Zoida), Haliyala, Dandeli.

Top attractions in uttarakannada
̥̥

MURDESHWARA

Located in Karnataka, this magnificent shrine is dedicated to Lord Shiva. This temple boasts of the second highest statue of Lord Shiva in the world, and the sheer magnanimity of the statue will fill you with a sense of awe. This temple is built on the Kanduka Hill which is surrounded by the Arabian Sea on three sides. The main temple's entrance, also called the 'gopura' stands at 123 feet and you can witness the breathtaking view of the Shiva statue from the top of the gopura.

DANDELI

Located on the Western Ghats of Uttara Karnataka, Dandeli is bestowed with the lush green forests and lies on the banks of the Kali river. Known for its many fantastic adventure opportunities, Dandeli is a hub for river rafting on river kali . Adventure seekers have many options such as mountain biking, trekking, cycling, kayaking and canoeing. The Dandeli Wildlife Sanctuary is another exciting element that Dandeli has to offer.

Mahabaleshwara Temple, Gokarna

A 6 feet tall Shiva Linga here at the temple is known as the Atmalinga is worshipped here. Built using white granite, it is a classic example of the beauty of Dravidian architecture. A 1500 years old carved stone Lord Shiva idol in the standing position represents the main deity. The temple has been mentioned in the Hindu Mythics of Mahabharata and Ramayana and it is said to be as significant as Kashi, hence it earning the title, Dakshin (South) Kashi.

Kasarkod Beach In Honnavar

This eco-friendly beach developed from the huge park in this village that was redeveloped by the forest committee of Kasarkod village, the forest department, and the tourism department. The beach was developed to encourage eco-tourism in Karnataka and to attract tourists from all over. The Kasarkod beach stretches up to a distance of 5 Kilometers. This virgin beach is covered in white sand and surrounded by Casuarinas. The scenic beauty and peace that surrounds Kasarkod offer a great experience to those who chose to walk along this beach.
